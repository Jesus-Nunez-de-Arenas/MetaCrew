The central theme of "The Peanuts Movie" revolves around the concepts of perseverance, self-discovery, and the universal quest for acceptance. It explores Charlie Brown’s struggles with self-doubt, his unyielding hope, and the courage to pursue his dreams despite frequent setbacks. Through his interactions with the Little Red-Haired Girl, Lucy, and his friends, the story highlights the importance of friendship, resilience in the face of adversity, and the value of believing in oneself. Ultimately, "The Peanuts Movie" conveys a heartfelt message that everyone faces challenges, and true fulfillment comes from embracing one’s individuality, learning from experiences, and maintaining a hopeful outlook, regardless of life's unpredictability.
