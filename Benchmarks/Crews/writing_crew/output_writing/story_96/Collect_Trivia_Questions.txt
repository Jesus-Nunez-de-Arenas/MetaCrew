Here are five trivia questions along with their correct answers that can be seamlessly integrated into a story reflecting the themes of family, belonging, and love, akin to those in "Lilo & Stitch":

1. **Question:** In computers, the X-Y Position Indicator for a Display System is now known as what?  
   **Answer:** "Mouse."

2. **Question:** The Khyber Pass is approximately how many miles long?  
   **Answer:** "33 miles."

3. **Question:** Which fruit did Nell Gwynn, mistress of King Charles II, sell in Drury Lane?  
   **Answer:** "Oranges."

4. **Question:** In the novel ‘Wuthering Heights’ by Emily Bronte, Catherine’s father found Heathcliff on the streets of which English city?  
   **Answer:** "Liverpool."

5. **Question:** Which Asian country hosted its first Formula One Grand Prix motor race in October 2010?  
   **Answer:** "India."

These trivia questions can enhance the narrative by introducing elements of cultural knowledge and historical context, tying together the core themes of "Lilo & Stitch" with the broader journey of understanding and acceptance, much like Lilo's efforts to teach Stitch about 'ohana' and the power of connection.
