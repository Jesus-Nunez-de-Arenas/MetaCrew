The central theme of "The Wizard of Oz" revolves around the journey of self-discovery and the quest for belonging. It follows Dorothy Gale, a young girl from Kansas, who is swept away by a tornado to the magical land of Oz. Throughout her adventures, Dorothy learns that the qualities she seeks—courage, love, and wisdom—are already within her and that true happiness lies in the connections we forge with others and the place we call home. The narrative emphasizes themes of friendship, bravery, and the importance of following one’s heart, culminating in the realization that "there's no place like home." Ultimately, "The Wizard of Oz" delivers a powerful message about the importance of self-acceptance and recognizing the strength we possess to overcome obstacles, reminding us that the journey to find ourselves often leads us back to where we began.
