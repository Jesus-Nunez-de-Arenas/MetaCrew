Here are five trivia questions along with their correct answers that can be seamlessly integrated into a story about adventure, self-discovery, and the connections we make along the journey:

1. **Question:** In which country was Earl Spencer's acrimonious divorce settlement heard?  
   **Answer:** The United Kingdom.

2. **Question:** Which of the founders of United Artists had the last name nearest to the beginning of the alphabet?  
   **Answer:** Charlie Chaplin.

3. **Question:** In "La Cage Aux Folles," what was La Cage Aux Folles?  
   **Answer:** A nightclub.

4. **Question:** What percentage of the Earth's surface is covered by Europe?  
   **Answer:** Approximately 2%.

5. **Question:** Which European town gave its name to a Treaty which symbolizes closer economic links between European countries?  
   **Answer:** Maastricht (referring to the Maastricht Treaty).

These trivia questions not only add interesting layers to the narrative but also resonate with themes of journeys and the search for identity, much like the adventures found in "The Wizard of Oz." Each question serves as a bridge to explore broader connections between people, places, and shared experiences.
