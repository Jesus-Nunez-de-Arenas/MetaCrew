Here are five trivia questions along with their correct answers that can be seamlessly integrated into a story that reflects the themes of environmentalism, connection, and the cautionary aspects of consumerism as seen in "WALL-E":

1. **Question:** Who was sacked as the Beatles' drummer and replaced by Ringo Starr?  
   **Answer:** Pete Best.

2. **Question:** Tsar Nicholas I abdicated in 1917, who was the other monarch to abdicate during this period?  
   **Answer:** Kaiser Wilhelm II of Germany.

3. **Question:** Whose last words were "Et tu, Brute?" according to Shakespeare?  
   **Answer:** Julius Caesar.

4. **Question:** Mel Gibson (1990) and Kenneth Branagh (1996) have both played which character in movies?  
   **Answer:** William Wallace (from "Braveheart") though it's related to Gibson, they portrayed different roles in their respective films.

5. **Question:** The original United Artists film studio was founded in 1919 by D.W. Griffith, Mary Pickford, Douglas Fairbanks, and who else?  
   **Answer:** Charlie Chaplin.

These trivia questions can be woven into the narrative of "WALL-E" to create engaging moments where characters reflect on various elements of history and art that parallel the film's exploration of humanity's impact on the environment and the essence of connection. By integrating these facts, the story not only entertains but also educates, emphasizing the importance of awareness and responsibility for our planet.
